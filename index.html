Hill Ave Multi-Use Path

Building a safer, greener, and more connected Fort Myers with a new 12-foot-wide multi-use path along Hill Avenue.

Explore the Project

Project Overview

The City of Fort Myers is advancing a multimodal transportation future with a 12-foot-wide, Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) compliant multi-use path on Hill Avenue. The project will provide a buffered, well-lit space for people who walk, use mobility devices, or ride bicycles while improving stormwater management and landscaping along the corridor.

Location

Hill Avenue between Colonial Boulevard and Winkler Avenue in central Fort Myers, FL.

Path Width

A continuous 12-foot paved path separated from vehicle traffic by new curbing, landscaped buffers, and lighting.

Project Goals

Enhance neighborhood connectivity, encourage active transportation, and expand safe routes to schools, parks, and employment centers.

Project Timeline

Planning & Design | Spring 2024

Finalize engineering plans, coordinate utility adjustments, and secure permits in consultation with neighborhood stakeholders.

Procurement | Summer 2024

Issue bid documents, select a contractor, and stage construction materials to minimize neighborhood disruptions.

Construction | Fall 2024 – Spring 2025

Install drainage, lighting, and the 12-foot multi-use path in phases to maintain access for residents and businesses.

Opening Celebration | Summer 2025

Host a community ribbon cutting with guided rides and walks highlighting the new connections along Hill Avenue.

Benefits for Fort Myers

Investing in connected pedestrian walkways and multi-use paths gives Fort Myers residents safer, healthier, and more affordable ways to commute. The Hill Avenue corridor is a vital link between neighborhoods, schools, and employment centers—this project will reduce the need for short car trips, ease congestion, and improve the city's air quality.

  • Creates a comfortable, protected space for people of all ages and abilities.
  • Supports active commuting options that save households on transportation costs.
  • Encourages walking and biking, improving public health outcomes across the city.
  • Strengthens connections to transit stops, parks, and local businesses.
  • Enhances neighborhood appeal with new lighting, landscaping, and stormwater features.
